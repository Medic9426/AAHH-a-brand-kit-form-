Questionnaire

This is where your brand starts. Take your time with each section — the more specific you are, the sharper the final kit will be.

Progress Section 1 of 12
Section 01

Your Brand Name & Tagline

Start with the basics — what the brand is called, exactly as it should appear everywhere.

Examples

"Creatures" — shape-shifting social space atop Otherland Galle.

"Wineyard" — never "The Wineyard." The name itself is the whole thing.

"Blindspot" — seven seats, no sign.

Section 02

Why This Place Exists

Not "we serve great drinks." The deeper reason — what this space is actually for.

Examples

"Creatures is not a place for people to 'go out.' It's a place to drop in — to themselves, to the rhythm, to something elevated."

"Cantina Futura isn't just about food — it's about understanding the complex web that connects our plates to the world."

Section 03

What You Are. What You're Not.

The most useful tool for staying on brand. Be specific — vague here makes everything harder later.

Example — Creatures

Not: A themed venue. No scarabs, no monsters, no faux jungle. Not an EDM club — music is curated, not chaotic. Not fine dining — the food is serious, not stiff.

Section 04

Brand Principles

3–5 things that, if they went missing, this place would stop being itself. Give each one a name.

Examples — Creatures

Sensory First — If it doesn't feel like something, it doesn't belong.

Mystery Over Clarity — We give just enough to let people make the story their own.

Raw Sophistication — Bohemian, not bourgeois. Luxe, but never sterile.

Section 05

Your Audience

Not demographics. Describe your guests like a person — what they do, what they care about, what a good night means to them.

Example

Primary: The after-work crowd. 28–40, works in media or creative industries. Wants the first drink to land hard and the second to be slower. Doesn't need to explain why they're here.

Secondary: Industry nights — bartenders and chefs on days off. Wants to be surprised, not sold to.

Section 06

Tone & Voice

How does this brand sound when it speaks? Not adjectives like "friendly" — something more specific. Give us real examples.

Examples

Wineyard: Warm but not eager. Dry wit. Never salesy.
Write like: "The kind of glass you have when you're not trying to impress anyone."
Never write like: "Join us for an unforgettable wine experience this Friday!!!"

Section 07

Brand Keywords

6–8 words that belong to this brand's world. Nouns, textures, emotions, times of day — not just adjectives.

Examples

Cantina Futura: Dialogue · Ecosystem · Heritage · Ingredients · Culinary · Innovation

Late-night bar: Amber · Smoke · Slow Pour · Ritual · Depth · After Midnight

Natural wine bar: Soil · Unfiltered · Conversation · Quiet · Process · Honest

Section 08

The Mood

Describe being in this space at its best — light, sound, temperature. Then share anything visual that captures it.

Example

Creatures: "The pause before the drop. Not the party — the anticipation of it. Red-orange light. Bass you feel before you hear it. The ocean somewhere behind everything."

Section 09

Color Palette

What colors already exist? Describe the feel — HEX codes if you have them, words if not.

Examples

Cantina Futura: Off-white canvas, deep charcoal text, warm amber accent.

Goa beach bar: Faded terracotta, bleached linen, deep ocean teal.

Section 10

Typography

Fonts are voice on paper. What typography feel suits this brand — and does anything already exist?

Examples

Creatures: IBM Plex Mono + IBM Plex Sans — technical precision meets approachable warmth.

Heritage bar: Weathered display font for headers, clean modern type for body.

Section 11

Your Logo

Tell us where the logo stands — what exists, what's needed, and any rules around how it should appear.

Example direction

Cantina Futura: "Sans-serif, heavy, readable. The type should be recognizable without the motif. Motifs are food-related in sketch style — black and white with a splash of color."

Section 12

Photography & Visual Style

The last section. What does great photography look like for this brand — and what would feel completely wrong?

Examples

Wineyard: Warm, intimate, natural light. Glasses with condensation. Unhurried. Never forced composition.

Creatures: Silhouettes, motion blur, light refraction. Never posed. The atmosphere is the subject, not the person.
